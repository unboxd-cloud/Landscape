# Governance

The landscape follows a simple governance model.

## Entry requirements

A project, protocol, vendor, or platform entry should include:

- stable name
- category
- architectural role
- maturity status
- homepage or source reference when available
- clear reason for inclusion

## Maturity labels

- `design`: concept or early planning
- `emerging`: early ecosystem adoption
- `active`: usable and actively maintained
- `incubating`: foundation or ecosystem incubation stage
- `graduated`: mature foundation-level project
- `archived`: inactive or retained for historical context

## Review principles

Entries should be accepted when they improve architectural clarity. Entries should be rejected when they are purely promotional, duplicated, abandoned, unsafe, or not relevant to the landscape.

## Decision posture

This landscape favors open standards, cloud-native operations, self-hostability, auditability, and clear governance boundaries.
